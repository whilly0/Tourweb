Olumo Rock, Ogun-State

Olumo Rock is a mountain in southwestern Nigeria, located in Abeokuta, Ogun State. Historically, it served as a natural fortress during inter-tribal wars in the 19th century. The name "Olumo" combines "Olu" (god/deity) and "mo" (moulded), emphasizing its cultural and spiritual significance. The rock has a height of 137 meters above sea level and is a popular tourist attraction.

My journey to Olumo Rock was an unforgettable adventure! Starting from Lagos, the trip took longer than expected due to traffic, but the destination was worth every minute. The site offers breathtaking views, historical landmarks, and a chance to connect with the past. Guided by a local, I climbed the rock, visited ancient caves, and learned about the area's fascinating history.
