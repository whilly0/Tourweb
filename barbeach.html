Bar Beach

; Bar Beach, situated along the Atlantic Ocean coastline in Victoria Island, Lagos, was one of the most popular beaches in Nigeria. It gained its name from the sandbars that formed a natural barrier, creating a lagoon between the beach and the open ocean. For many years, Bar Beach served as a recreational hub for Lagosians and visitors alike. It was a vibrant and bustling destination where families gathered for picnics, friends enjoyed beach sports, and vendors sold snacks and souvenirs along the shoreline. However, the history of Bar Beach is also marked by significant changes and challenges. Over time, erosion and coastal development altered the landscape, diminishing the beach's size and affecting its appeal as a recreational spot. Additionally, pollution became a growing concern, impacting the water quality and detracting from the beach experience. In recent years, efforts have been made to revitalize the area and address environmental concerns. The Lagos State government initiated projects to reclaim land and expand the waterfront, aiming to create new leisure and commercial spaces along the coastline.

; Despite its transformation and challenges, Bar Beach remains a symbol of Lagos's cultural heritage and coastal identity. Located in Ekiti State, Ikogosi is situated in a dense forest and extends over a hilly landscape. Many stories have been told as regards the history of this site. The most striking of all the tales is that the warm and cold water were wives of a man who, after a long contest, turned into springs through magical means. The troublesome wife became the warm water, while the gentle and peace-loving wife became the cold water. However, it is largely believed that the spring was discovered by a Baptist Missionary in 1950. Asides the spectacular waterfall where warm and cold water flow side by side, Ikogosi has a well-designed trail that leads to the meeting point of cold and warm water which is guaranteed to amaze nature lovers.
